FAQ

Questions before you start.

Straight answers for marketing leaders and brand teams. Anything else, write to hello@perceptivity.ai.

What's the difference between SEO and AEO/GEO?
SEO optimises for a list of ten blue links. AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) and GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) optimise for a single AI-generated answer. When a buyer asks ChatGPT or Gemini, the engine reads the web, forms a verdict, and names one brand. Showing up at rank 6 on Google doesn't show up at all in that answer.
Do you actually run real queries on ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude and the others?
Yes — in production we run thousands of category buyer questions across all seven engines, refreshed daily. This site simulates the scan so you can see the experience without sharing credentials. The flow, the data shape and the model are exactly what production looks like.
What counts as a "buyer query"?
One unique prompt sent to one engine. So "best electric SUV under ₹30 lakhs" sent to ChatGPT, Perplexity and Gemini counts as three queries. During onboarding we help you build a smart, high-intent query list so you're not wasting volume on low-value prompts.
How is this different from existing AEO monitoring tools?
Monitoring tools tell you AI mentioned your brand — a lagging signal. Perceptivity tells you which campaign will change the answer before you commit budget (Simulate), gives you an execution queue with owners and partners (Act), then measures predicted vs actual lift (Learn). The full loop, not half of it.
Is this a global platform, or India-only?
Global. Perceptivity runs on regional answer graphs — local buyer language, publishers and competitive sets — for every market we model. India is where our category depth runs deepest today, which is why the worked examples here are Indian brands; the platform itself rolls out market by market.
Is my data safe?
Your content, campaigns and outcomes are encrypted at rest and in transit, stored in your own tenant, and never used to improve other customers' results. Enterprise plans include SSO and audit logs; SOC 2 Type II is on the roadmap.
